# GRAD521_downenDMP_2026

Dungeness crab is the largest and most commecially valuable fishery on the West Coast
of the United States. However, fisheries along the West Coast have been increasingly
impacted by harmful algal blooms (HABs), causing fisheries closures and massive finan-
cial losses. Harmful algal bloom events have been successfully modeled in California via
the California Harmful Algae Risk Mapping (C-HARM) system. My current research
is focused on developing index insurance products for Dungeness Crab in California for
fisheries that are impacted by harmful algae blooms using the event models. Our primary
research question is: What is the feasibility and effectiveness of index insurance products
based on the C-HARM model as a financial risk management solution for losses resulting
from fisheries closures?
